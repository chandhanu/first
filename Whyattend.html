Why is such a workshop on fundamentals required now?

It's buzzing hot, it's truly interdisciplinary and it's much more than just numbers: Data Analytics and all the related terms including Data Mining, Data Warehousing, Data Sciences, Big Data, and so on, have emerged as important subjects of study across a large variety of disciplines spanning the Arts, Business, Commerce, Humanities, Information and Natural Sciences, and Social Sciences.

What you don't know that you don't know could hurt more:

The processes of identification, collection, storage, encoding, retrieval and analysis of data, of all forms, formats, and sizes, and the sharing of the results thereof in all these disciplines are ubiquitous and important to users more than any time before.

We're talking about competition and success, not mere survival:

In our hyper-competitive world, the insights gained from proper and distinctive data analysis can prove to be the most important difference between the finest success and ordinary survival.

You better know the fundamentals before diving into the deep-end:

Across our country, many such Workshops, and other academic events, focused on the deep-end of Data Analytics and on the technologies, continue to be organized. It is hence necessary to orient and guide young teacherresearchers, research scholars and post-graduate students towards the fundamentals of Data Analysis and Analytics, by providing them with a thorough grounding in the subject, and preparing them to dive into the deep-end and emerge confidently and successfully.
